Key points

  • CDC reported 1,671 confirmed measles cases in 2026 as of April 2, with 94 percent of cases linked to outbreaks.
  • MMR coverage among kindergartners fell to 92.5 percent in 2024–25, down from 95.2 percent in 2019–20, leaving roughly 286,000 without complete documentation.
  • Measles is extraordinarily contagious: up to 9 in 10 susceptible close contacts will become infected, while two doses of MMR are about 97 percent effective.
  • The problem is not a lack of scientific tools. It is whether vaccination, recordkeeping, access, and rapid response remain ordinary enough to work.
