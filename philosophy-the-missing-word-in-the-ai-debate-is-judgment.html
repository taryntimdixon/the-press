Key points

  • International and national AI frameworks already exist, including the OECD AI Principles, UNESCO’s ethics recommendation, and NIST’s AI Risk Management Framework.
  • Those frameworks matter, but they do not eliminate the need for human institutions to interpret cases, weigh trade-offs, and answer for consequences.
  • Hannah Arendt’s account of thinking, judgment, and “enlarged mentality” offers a useful lens for public decisions about AI.
  • The real governance danger is not only technical error. It is procedural numbness: institutions acting as though a model output settles questions that still demand human judgment.
