Key points

  • CDC continues to describe current public-health risk as low, while tracking 71 U.S. human cases since February 2024.
  • USDA’s National Milk Testing Strategy now treats dairy surveillance as a core national defense against H5N1 spread.
  • FDA says 464 pasteurized dairy products tested negative for viable H5N1 and that pasteurization inactivates the virus.
  • Worker protection and farm biosecurity are not side issues; they are part of the public-health perimeter.
