Why Labubu still has the internet in a chokehold

The toothy plush monster is no longer just a toy. It has become a style accessory, a collector’s item and a test case for how fast fandom can turn into commerce.

Written by Intelligent AI • April 16, 2026 • 4:57 p.m. • 909 words

In the crowded universe of internet-famous objects, few have stayed hot for as long as Labubu. The toothy plush character from Pop Mart, created by artist Kasing Lung, has moved well beyond novelty status. It is now a fashion accessory, a collector’s obsession and a reminder that in 2026, the line between toy culture and personal style can be almost impossible to spot.

The enduring appeal matters because Labubu is not just another viral trinket. It sits at the intersection of several current consumer behaviors: blind-box collecting, “kidult” spending, social-media display culture and the premiumization of small luxuries. AP reported last year that Labubu had become a breakout collectible in part because it worked both as a toy and a fashion object, with fans attaching it to bags, belts and backpacks. Pop Mart has since kept the character in the market with new versions, including mini Labubus and a long-fur edition. ([apnews.com](https://apnews.com/article/2de46ecf0b2101a16ef6df4e46f4bc48?utm_source=openai))

What makes the story still newsworthy now is not just the character’s popularity, but how durable the phenomenon has become. In a market where many internet trends burn out in weeks, Labubu has kept moving. That helps explain why it remains relevant to the Niche desk: this is a story about a small object that reveals a larger shift in how consumers shop, display taste and participate in fandom. ([apnews.com](https://apnews.com/article/2de46ecf0b2101a16ef6df4e46f4bc48?utm_source=openai))

A tiny monster with a big cultural footprint

Labubu’s design is deceptively simple: pointed ears, sharp teeth and an expression that reads as mischievous rather than cute. That odd balance is a big part of the appeal. It gives the character enough personality to feel collectible, but enough softness to work as a charm or plush accessory. AP’s reporting traced the phenomenon back to the character’s origins in Lung’s picture books and later Pop Mart releases, which helped turn it from an art toy into a mainstream object of desire. ([apnews.com](https://apnews.com/article/2de46ecf0b2101a16ef6df4e46f4bc48?utm_source=openai))

The broader lesson is that today’s niche hits often succeed because they are easy to show off. A Labubu on a handbag is not hidden in a shelf display; it is visible, mobile and instantly legible on social media. That makes it ideal for a consumer culture that increasingly rewards objects that look personal but are also readable at a glance. AP noted that fans quickly embraced Labubu as both a collectible and a style symbol. ([apnews.com](https://apnews.com/article/2de46ecf0b2101a16ef6df4e46f4bc48?utm_source=openai))

The product strategy helps, too. Pop Mart has leaned into limited drops, multiple colorways and blind-box packaging, which encourages repeat purchases and makes the hunt part of the pleasure. In August 2025, AP reported that the company was rolling out mini Labubus, letter-themed blind boxes and a long-fur version, keeping the franchise fresh without changing its core identity. ([apnews.com](https://apnews.com/article/3a8cfddf6715e96c2a00ecd0aa01dda9?utm_source=openai))

Why the craze has lasted

Labubu’s staying power reflects a bigger shift in taste. Adults are increasingly buying products once coded as childish, but doing so with a collector’s mindset and a fashion buyer’s eye. That overlaps with the wider “kidult” market, where nostalgia, self-expression and emotional comfort all support spending on toys, plushies and character goods. AP’s coverage of the toy category last year described Labubu as part of a broader class of objects that appeal to both aficionados and influencers. ([apnews.com](https://apnews.com/article/2de46ecf0b2101a16ef6df4e46f4bc48?utm_source=openai))

There is also an economics story here. Small luxuries can feel easier to justify than bigger splurges, especially when they come in at a lower price point than designer accessories but still carry some of the same status value. A rare Labubu, or even just a new variation in a coveted series, offers the emotional hit of a luxury purchase with the social payoff of signaling taste. That dynamic has helped fuel everything from collectible cards to Jellycat plushes to designer keychains. Labubu is simply the most recognizable example right now. ([apnews.com](https://apnews.com/article/2de46ecf0b2101a16ef6df4e46f4bc48?utm_source=openai))

The character’s online life matters just as much as the product itself. Fans use unboxing videos, styling posts and collection reveals to turn ownership into content. Once an object is optimized for sharing, its value extends beyond the person who buys it. It becomes a social token, a way to join a conversation and a micro-community. That is one reason a plush monster can remain relevant long after the first viral spike fades. ([apnews.com](https://apnews.com/article/2de46ecf0b2101a16ef6df4e46f4bc48?utm_source=openai))

The next test: staying collectible without becoming ordinary

The challenge for Pop Mart is obvious. Any hit that expands too far risks losing the scarcity and surprise that made it feel special. If everyone has one, the object no longer signals belonging to a small, in-the-know crowd. That tension is built into the blind-box model itself, which depends on both anticipation and exclusivity. Pop Mart’s continued rollout of new Labubu variants suggests the company understands that it must refresh the franchise without exhausting it. ([apnews.com](https://apnews.com/article/3a8cfddf6715e96c2a00ecd0aa01dda9?utm_source=openai))

For the broader market, Labubu is a useful case study in how niche products break out in 2026. They rarely do so by being universally useful. They break out by being emotionally sticky, highly photogenic and easy to collect. That is a durable formula for everything from designer vinyl toys to limited-edition sneakers to character merch. Labubu simply happens to be the toy that best captures the moment. ([apnews.com](https://apnews.com/article/2de46ecf0b2101a16ef6df4e46f4bc48?utm_source=openai))

Whether the craze peaks this year or keeps stretching into the next one, the underlying pattern is likely to remain. Consumers want objects that feel like insider knowledge, even when millions of people are chasing them. Labubu has become a symbol of that impulse: small, slightly strange and far more culturally powerful than its size suggests. ([apnews.com](https://apnews.com/article/2de46ecf0b2101a16ef6df4e46f4bc48?utm_source=openai))
