CinemaCon 2026’s biggest surprise: Amazon MGM turns to nostalgia, star power and a bigger theatrical swing

The studio used its Las Vegas showcase to unveil new looks at Spaceballs 2, The Thomas Crown Affair and more, signaling how aggressively it wants to grow its movie slate after Project Hail Mary’s breakout.

Written by Intelligent AI • April 17, 2026 • 11:57 a.m. • 1171 words

LAS VEGAS — At CinemaCon, where studio presentations often blur together into a blur of trailers, release dates and applause cues, Amazon MGM Studios managed to make itself the conversation. The company’s showcase on Wednesday leaned hard into theatrical ambition, pairing a newly revealed title for the long-awaited Spaceballs sequel with first looks at Michael B. Jordan’s The Thomas Crown Affair, Peter Farrelly’s I Play Rocky and other upcoming releases. The message was clear: Amazon MGM is no longer treating movies as an occasional prestige play. It wants to be a bigger force at the multiplex. ([apnews.com](https://apnews.com/article/bcf2a4e6d3e4f226115ca0d1505b350f))

The timing matters. Amazon MGM arrives at CinemaCon on the back of a genuine breakout in Project Hail Mary, which has already become the studio’s biggest theatrical hit and one of the year’s top box-office earners. AP reported this week that the sci-fi film had crossed $515 million globally, and that it was strong enough to earn a return engagement in IMAX theaters. For a studio still building an identity on the big screen, that kind of performance is both a proof of concept and a pressure point: if one original title can travel that well, the market for more theatrical bets may be wider than skeptics assumed. ([apnews.com](https://apnews.com/article/bcf2a4e6d3e4f226115ca0d1505b350f?utm_source=openai))

Spaceballs returns with a title and a tone

The headline grabber was the Spaceballs sequel. Mel Brooks appeared via video to announce the title Spaceballs: The New One, a name that fits the original 1987 spoof’s anything-goes attitude and its contempt for self-importance. The presentation in Las Vegas also brought Bill Pullman and Rick Moranis to the stage, underscoring how seriously Amazon MGM is treating a comedy franchise that has lived for decades more as cult memory than active property. The studio framed the project with exactly the kind of irreverence fans would expect, while also giving exhibitors an unmistakable signal that legacy comedy can still be part of a theatrical rollout in 2026. ([apnews.com](https://apnews.com/article/bcf2a4e6d3e4f226115ca0d1505b350f))

That choice is notable because nostalgia alone is not usually enough to justify a wide-release theatrical sequel. But Spaceballs is not just any legacy title; it is a satire with built-in audience affection, and the return of Moranis is especially meaningful because the actor has largely stayed out of live-action filmmaking for years. The studio’s wager is that the movie can serve both as a generational callback and as a fresh comedically self-aware event. In an era when comedy is often pushed toward streaming, Amazon MGM is betting that a familiar brand and a theatrical crowd can still generate buzz in the room, not just online. ([apnews.com](https://apnews.com/article/bcf2a4e6d3e4f226115ca0d1505b350f))

Michael B. Jordan’s Thomas Crown puts style back in play

If Spaceballs sold the room on broad fan appeal, The Thomas Crown Affair sold it on polish. Jordan, who is both directing and starring in the film, described the project as a long-held ambition and said the 1999 version made a strong impression on him when he was 12. He also said he wants to preserve the style, sophistication and rebellion associated with the earlier films while making the lead character someone audiences can root for. AP reported that the presentation included an introduction from Jordan and a performance from Jon Batiste, who is composing the score. ([apnews.com](https://apnews.com/article/bcf2a4e6d3e4f226115ca0d1505b350f))

That combination suggests Amazon MGM is trying to position the film as more than a remake. The original Thomas Crown Affair films have always been about charisma, wealth and risk as much as plot mechanics, and Jordan’s version appears designed to turn those ingredients into a modern star vehicle. The casting of Adria Arjona points to a sleek romantic-crime register, while the presence of Batiste hints that the studio wants the movie to feel fashionable in the same way a studio thriller once could. In an exhibition marketplace hungry for films that feel event-sized without relying on capes or familiar IP alone, that matters. ([apnews.com](https://apnews.com/article/bcf2a4e6d3e4f226115ca0d1505b350f))

A bigger Amazon MGM, not just a louder one

The broader significance of the presentation lies in scale. Amazon MGM told exhibitors it plans to release 15 movies a year by 2027, an ambitious target that would move the company closer to the rhythm of a traditional major studio. That is a notable shift for a company better known in recent years for prestige streaming strategy than for a steady theatrical cadence. CinemaCon is often where studios pitch confidence as much as product, but Amazon MGM’s slate made a practical argument: it has a hit in Project Hail Mary, an established brand in Spaceballs, a glossy star-driven caper in The Thomas Crown Affair, and a mix of action, family and biographical titles to fill out the calendar. ([apnews.com](https://apnews.com/article/bcf2a4e6d3e4f226115ca0d1505b350f))

The studio also previewed Masters of the Universe, The Sheep Detectives, How to Rob a Bank, Highlander and the Colleen Hoover adaptation Verity, a lineup that spans nostalgia, youth appeal, action and literary fandom. That range suggests a simple strategy: use known titles where they help, but do not rely on only one genre or one audience segment. For theater owners, the appeal is obvious. A studio that can move between franchise, adult-skewing spectacle and family-friendly fare can supply more consistent box-office inventory, which is exactly what exhibitors want from a partner in a still-fragile theatrical market. ([apnews.com](https://apnews.com/article/bcf2a4e6d3e4f226115ca0d1505b350f))

The Bond question hangs over the room

What Amazon MGM did not have was nearly as revealing. The studio had no new Bond update to share, despite owning the franchise’s future and having already tapped Denis Villeneuve to direct the first film of the new era. AP reported that the company repeated its intention to approach the property with care, but the lack of concrete movement is notable against the relative clarity elsewhere in the presentation. If Spaceballs and Thomas Crown were proof of motion, Bond was proof of caution. ([apnews.com](https://apnews.com/article/bcf2a4e6d3e4f226115ca0d1505b350f))

That caution may be strategic. Bond is one of the few properties in Hollywood whose next step is itself a major piece of news, and any premature reveal could overshadow the relaunch. But in the immediate term, the silence also reinforces the idea that Amazon MGM is willing to build its theatrical reputation on more than one mega-franchise. It is trying to become the kind of studio that can launch original hits, revive dormant series and still keep one of the most valuable spy brands in cinema in reserve. For exhibitors watching the market reel from one franchise cycle to the next, that’s the more interesting story: not just that Amazon MGM can make headlines, but that it appears determined to become indispensable. ([apnews.com](https://apnews.com/article/bcf2a4e6d3e4f226115ca0d1505b350f))

After Wednesday night, CinemaCon had a new studio to watch. Amazon MGM did not simply show off footage; it outlined a business plan in public, one built on theatrical confidence, recognizable IP and a willingness to spend real energy on the kind of moviegoing experience studios once took for granted. If Project Hail Mary was the proof, and Spaceballs: The New One the punchline, then Amazon MGM’s 2026 presentation was the promise that there is more to come. ([apnews.com](https://apnews.com/article/bcf2a4e6d3e4f226115ca0d1505b350f))
