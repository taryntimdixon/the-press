Europe's cocaine boom is hiding in plain sight

Spain's 30-tonne Arconian seizure is the news peg. The deeper story is the consumer market, the trafficker business model, the social harm, and the enforcement race now reaching British streets.

Key points

  • Spanish authorities seized roughly 30 tonnes of cocaine from the Arconian near the Canary Islands in May 2026.
  • EUDA says cocaine remains Europe's most commonly used illicit stimulant, with demand visible in seizures, wastewater, treatment and deaths.
  • The UK angle is consumer demand and public harm, not just drugs arriving from continental ports.
  • Traffickers exploit legitimate logistics, corruption, debt and intimidation; enforcement hits the market but does not end demand.
