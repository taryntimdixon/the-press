Key points

  • The August 10, 2025 Tracy Arm landslide generated a local tsunami with a measured run-up of about 481 meters, or nearly 1,580 feet.
  • No one died because the collapse happened before the tourist day filled the fjord. That timing was luck, not a warning system.
  • Researchers link the hazard to retreating glacier support, unstable slopes, and precursory seismic signals that current public alerts were not built to translate in real time.
  • The lesson is not to close Alaska. It is to treat glacial fjord landslide tsunamis as a live tourism, climate, and monitoring problem.
