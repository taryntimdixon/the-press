Key points

  • Elon Musk says OpenAI abandoned the nonprofit mission it used to attract early support.
  • OpenAI says Musk wanted control, failed to get it, left, founded xAI, and is now attacking a competitor.
  • The trial centers on breach of charitable trust, unjust enrichment, and Microsoft’s role in OpenAI’s commercial rise.
  • The bigger question is whether public-interest AI can survive private capital at frontier scale.

Why it matters

The case is not only a billionaire feud. It could shape how future AI labs use nonprofit missions, public benefit structures, investor money, and commercial partnerships while claiming to build systems for the public good.
