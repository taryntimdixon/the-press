The Pledge

We will tell you how this paper is made before asking you to trust it.

The Press is built around That is not a shameful footnote. It is the central fact of the publication. The machine helps with speed, structure, synthesis, and first drafts. The editor is responsible for what appears on the page.

AI can write clean sentences while being confidently wrong, which is a charming little nightmare humanity invented and then connected to every workplace. So our standard is simple: no claim should stand unless it can be traced back to a source, a record, a dataset, a direct statement, or a clearly labeled inference.

We are not asking you to believe the article because the prose sounds polished. We are asking you to inspect the evidence, follow the links, compare the claims, and decide for yourself.

Behind the Scenes

What happens before an article goes live.

The glossy page is the end of the process. The work underneath is less glamorous and much more important.

  1. 01

    Story radar

    We look for subjects where the public record has moved: official releases, data, filings, studies, transcripts, and credible reporting.

  2. 02

    Source bundle

    The article starts with receipts. The draft should be tied to documents the editor and reader can open, not vibes in a trench coat.

  3. 03

    AI draft

    The Press turns the source bundle into a structured draft, with the byline and AI disclosure treated as part of the story, not an afterthought.

  4. 04

    Claim audit

    Names, dates, numbers, causal claims, and quotes are checked against the source trail. Unsupported certainty gets cut.

  5. 05

    Human edit

    The editor reads for accuracy, tone, overreach, missing context, and the classic AI disease of sounding smarter than it has earned.

  6. 06

    Publish with receipts

    Stories keep source notes, credits, and context close to the article so readers can audit the work instead of worshiping the output.

  7. 07

    Correct in public

    When something is wrong, it belongs on the corrections page with a date, a plain explanation, and no theatrical fog machine.

Receipts, Not Mysticism

The source trail is part of the product.

Primary records first

Government documents, datasets, court records, transcripts, official statements, and original research carry more weight than commentary.

Links readers can follow

The goal is not to sound authoritative. The goal is to make the authority checkable by someone who does not work here.

Clear uncertainty

When the evidence is partial, developing, disputed, or messy, the story should say that instead of cosplaying as a crystal ball.

The Fine Print, Out Loud

What we will do, and what we will not pretend.

We will

  • Disclose AI authorship clearly on the Masthead page and in publication language.
  • Build articles around sources that can be inspected.
  • Keep a human editor responsible for the final published page.
  • Separate verified facts from analysis, interpretation, and uncertainty.
  • Correct meaningful errors publicly and plainly.

We will not

  • Invent quotes, sources, experts, documents, statistics, or anonymous whispers from nowhere.
  • Pretend a model is a person with lived reporting experience.
  • Hide the use of AI behind soft little euphemisms built by committee gremlins.
  • Publish a claim just because the sentence sounds confident.
  • Ask you to trust the machine. Check the machine.

Reader Advisory

Read at your own risk. Form your own conclusions.

We can review, source, edit, label, and correct. We cannot think for you, and honestly that would be a terrible product feature. Treat every article as an argument with evidence attached. Open the sources. Compare the record. Notice what is missing. Push back when the story overreaches.
