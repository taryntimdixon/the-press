Key points

  • Federal statistics are not clerical side work; they are the information infrastructure that lets a continental democracy govern itself.
  • Official directives explicitly tie the statistical system to objectivity, independence, confidentiality, and prompt public release.
  • Recent Census releases on the ACS and AIES show that measurement must evolve with the economy and the country it describes.
  • When trust in public numbers erodes, politics does not become more authentic; it becomes more improvisational and more captive to mood.
