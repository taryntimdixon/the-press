Key points

  • The March 31 White House order pairs citizenship verification with new mail-ballot controls and federal enforcement pressure.
  • The real hinge is the national voter-registration form and whether attestation remains the federal baseline or documentary proof becomes the front-door rule.
  • The EAC’s archive shows this fight predates the current order; states previously tried to add extra proof-of-citizenship requirements to the federal form.
  • The states now in court argue the order would force rushed system changes before federal elections and unlawfully interfere with state election administration.
