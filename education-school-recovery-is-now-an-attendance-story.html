Key points

  • Fourth-grade reading in 2024 was lower than both 2022 and 2019, while fourth-grade math improved from 2022 but remained below 2019.
  • Chronic absenteeism fell from its pandemic-era peak but remained extraordinarily high, with twenty states still above 30 percent in 2022–23.
  • More than a quarter of public-school leaders said lack of focus or inattention had a severe negative effect on learning in 2023–24.
  • Tutoring helps, but recovery still depends on attendance, staffing, and the daily instructional continuity schools are struggling to rebuild.

How this story was built

This story was built from current federal education indicators, including NCES Condition of Education reporting, 2024 NAEP national results, Department of Education absenteeism guidance, NCES School Pulse findings on attention and tutoring, and NCES staffing and teacher-vacancy data.
