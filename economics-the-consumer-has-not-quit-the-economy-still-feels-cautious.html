Key points

  • March payroll growth and a 4.3 percent unemployment rate do not describe collapse, but the labor market is clearly less dynamic than it was earlier in the cycle.
  • Consumer spending and retail sales remain one of the broadest supports under the expansion, even as inflation and caution persist.
  • Hiring has cooled materially: February job openings were steady, but hires fell to 4.8 million and the hires rate slipped to its lowest point since April 2020.
  • Construction and industrial investment are still providing support, but the economy now looks more like endurance than ease.

How this story was built

This piece was built from current federal economic releases, including the March employment report, January personal income and outlays, February retail and labor-turnover data, current construction and productivity releases, the Federal Reserve’s March statement, and the Atlanta Fed’s GDPNow estimate.
