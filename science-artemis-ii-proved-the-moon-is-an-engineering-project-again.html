Key points

  • Artemis II launched on April 1 for an approximately 10-day crewed lunar flyby, the first time humans left Earth orbit since Apollo 17.
  • The mission is fundamentally an operations test: crewed Orion performance, power, navigation, communications, recovery, and deep-space procedures.
  • NASA said the crew set a new record for farthest distance traveled by humans in space, underscoring how different cislunar operations are from low Earth orbit.
  • The mission also demonstrates that lunar exploration now depends on multinational hardware and industrial continuity, not nostalgia alone.
