Key points

  • BEA says arts and cultural activity accounted for 4.2% of U.S. GDP in 2023 and grew faster than the broader economy.
  • The NEA’s 2024 pulse data shows real participation: about one-third of adults reported some recent arts attendance, with a quarter attending a live performance or exhibit.
  • Broadway’s 2024–25 season set a gross record, but industry leaders still warn about rising costs and shorter windows for productions.
  • The national challenge is durability and local access: a rebound at the top does not guarantee healthy middle-tier and community cultural systems.

How this story was built

This story combines federal economic and participation data from BEA and the NEA with Broadway League season and weekly attendance statistics to ask not whether culture returned, but what kind of recovery actually took hold.
